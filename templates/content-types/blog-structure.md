# Blog Post Structure Template — ContentForge

## Content Type: Blog Post
**Target Word Count:** 800-1500 words
**Target Reading Level:** Flesch-Kincaid Grade 8-10
**Minimum Citations:** 5-8 sources
**SEO Focus:** Medium-High (primary + 2-3 secondary keywords)
**Tone:** More conversational than articles, personality-driven

---

## Standard Structure

### 1. Title (H1)
- **Format:** Catchy, conversational, benefit-driven, includes primary keyword
- **Length:** 40-60 characters (shorter than articles for punchier impact)
- **Style:** Often uses numbers, questions, or power words
- **Examples:**
  - "[Number] [Primary Keyword] Tips That Actually Work"
  - "Why [Primary Keyword] Is Harder (and Easier) Than You Think"
  - "I Tried [Primary Keyword] for 30 Days. Here's What Happened."
  - "The [Adjective] Truth About [Primary Keyword]"

### 2. Introduction (100-150 words)
**Purpose:** Quick hook, establish relatability, get to the point fast

**Components:**
1. **Opening Hook** (1-2 sentences)
   - Personal anecdote, surprising fact, or direct question
   - More casual than article openings
   - Primary keyword natural, not forced

2. **Relate to Reader** (2-3 sentences)
   - "You've probably experienced..."
   - "If you're like most [audience], you..."
   - Show empathy or shared challenge

3. **Promise** (1 sentence)
   - What this post delivers
   - Keep it simple and direct

**SEO Requirements:**
- Primary keyword in first 100 words ✓
- Conversational tone ✓

---

### 3. Main Body (500-1000 words)
**Structure:** 3-5 major sections, each with H2 heading

#### Section Pattern (Repeat 3-5 times)

**H2 Heading:**
- Conversational, often uses "How to..." or "Why..." or "[Number] Ways..."
- Can ask questions
- Examples:
  - "What Makes [Topic] So Challenging?"
  - "3 Mistakes Everyone Makes With [Topic]"
  - "The One Thing That Changed Everything"

**Content (150-250 words per section):**
1. **Lead-In** — Conversational opening, often uses "Here's the thing..." or "So..."
2. **Main Point** — The core idea explained simply
3. **Evidence/Story** — Data, personal experience, or quick example (with citation if factual claim)
4. **Takeaway** — "What this means for you..." or "Bottom line:"

**H3 Subheadings (Use Sparingly):**
- Only if section is complex
- Keep casual: "But Wait, There's More" or "Here's Why This Matters"

**Content Elements to Include:**
- [ ] Personal voice ("I", "we", "you")
- [ ] Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences)
- [ ] Occasional questions to reader
- [ ] Real examples (not generic)
- [ ] Stats when available (with citations)
- [ ] Contractions (it's, you're, don't) — unless brand prohibits

**Formatting:**
- Paragraphs: 1-3 sentences (blog readers scan more)
- Bullet lists: Common, for quick tips or key points
- Numbered lists: For step-by-step or ranked items
- Bold: Key phrases or important words
- Occasional sentence fragments. For emphasis.
- Dashes for asides — like this — or parentheticals

---

### 4. Actionable Section (100-200 words)
**H2:** "Try This:" or "Your Next Steps" or "Quick Wins to Start Today"

**Purpose:** Make it immediately useful

**Format Options:**

**Option A: Simple Action Steps**
- Do [X] to get [outcome]
- Try [Y] if [situation]
- Avoid [Z] because [reason]

**Option B: Quick Checklist**
✓ [Action item 1]
✓ [Action item 2]
✓ [Action item 3]

**Option C: "Start Here" Mini-Guide**
1. [First, do this]
2. [Then, do this]
3. [Finally, this]

---

### 5. Conclusion (80-120 words)

**Components:**
1. **Circle Back** (2-3 sentences)
   - Reference opening hook or problem
   - Reinforce main point
   - Keep it conversational

2. **Leave Them Thinking** (1-2 sentences)
   - Question, challenge, or forward-looking statement
   - More open-ended than article conclusions

3. **Soft CTA** (1 sentence)
   - "What's worked for you? Let me know in the comments."
   - "Want more tips like this? Subscribe to our weekly newsletter."
   - "Check out our guide on [related topic] next."

**SEO Requirements:**
- Primary keyword appears naturally ✓
- Internal link to related content ✓

---

### 6. References (Minimal Formatting)

**Format:** Simple numbered list or footnote-style

**Style:** APA or simplified citation (per brand)

**Example (Simplified):**
1. Stat about X from [Source Name], 2026
2. Research on Y from [Author/Organization], available at [URL]

**Requirements:**
- Include for any factual claims or statistics
- Keep formatting simple (this isn't an academic paper)
- Links can be inline in text instead of separate bibliography

## SEO Optimization Checklist

**Title & Headings:**
- [ ] Primary keyword in title (H1)
- [ ] Primary keyword in at least 1 H2 heading
- [ ] Secondary keywords in 1-2 H2 headings (if natural)
- [ ] Headings are descriptive and enticing

**Keyword Density:**
- [ ] Primary keyword: 1.5-2.0% of total word count
- [ ] Secondary keywords: 0.5-1% each
- [ ] Natural language (blog voice preserved)

**Meta Elements:**
- [ ] Meta title: ≤60 characters, conversational, includes primary keyword
- [ ] Meta description: 150-155 characters, engaging, includes primary keyword + hook
- [ ] URL slug: short, includes primary keyword

**Content Structure:**
- [ ] Primary keyword in first 100 words
- [ ] Primary keyword in conclusion
- [ ] 2-4 internal links to related blog posts or resources
- [ ] 5-8 external citations (fewer than articles, but still credible)
- [ ] Scannable (short paragraphs, lists, subheadings)

**Engagement Elements:**
- [ ] At least 1 question to reader
- [ ] Clear, actionable takeaways
- [ ] CTA (subscribe, comment, share, download)

---

## Quality Standards

**Readability:**
- Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level: 8-10 (middle school to early high school)
- Flesch Reading Ease: 60-70 (standard, fairly easy)
- Average sentence length: 12-18 words
- Paragraphs: 1-3 sentences (2-4 lines on screen)

**Citation Standards:**
- Minimum 5 citations (for blogs with factual claims)
- Fewer required than articles
- Can be inline links instead of formal bibliography
- Stats and data must still be sourced

**Brand Compliance:**
- Voice and tone match brand profile
- Can be more casual and personal than articles
- Use contractions and direct address (unless brand prohibits)

---

## Blog-Specific Best Practices

✅ **Do:**
- Write like you talk
- Use "I" and "you" freely
- Ask questions throughout
- Share personal experiences or client stories
- Start sentences with "And" or "But" occasionally
- Use short paragraphs (even 1 sentence)
- Include a clear CTA
- Make it scannable (lists, bold, subheadings)

❌ **Don't:**
- Sound overly formal or academic
- Use jargon without explanation
- Write long paragraphs (>4 sentences)
- Bury the point (get to it fast)
- Ignore SEO basics (still needs keywords, meta tags)
- Skip citations for claims (still needs credibility)
- End without clear next step

## Example Blog Outline

**Title:** "5 SEO Mistakes That Are Killing Your Traffic (And How to Fix Them)"

**Introduction (120 words)**
- Hook: "I spent 6 months writing 'perfect' blog posts. Traffic? Zero."
- Relate: "If your content isn't showing up in Google, you're not alone..."
- Promise: "Here are the 5 mistakes I fixed — and saw traffic jump 300% in 90 days."

**H2: Mistake #1: Ignoring Search Intent (180 words)**
- What it is
- Why it kills traffic (with stat + citation)
- Quick fix
- Personal example

**H2: Mistake #2: Keyword Stuffing (Like It's 2010) (180 words)**
- What it looks like
- Why Google hates it (citation to algorithm updates)
- How to use keywords naturally
- Before/after example

**H2: Mistake #3: Writing for Robots, Not Humans (200 words)**
- The readability problem
- Data on dwell time + SEO (citation)
- How to balance SEO + readability
- My simple test: read it aloud

**H2: Mistake #4: Zero Internal Links (150 words)**
- Why internal linking matters (SEO + UX)
- How to do it without being spammy
- Quick win: add 2-3 relevant links to every post

**H2: Mistake #5: Forgetting Mobile Readers (150 words)**
- Mobile traffic stats (citation)
- Short paragraphs, scannable format
- Test: view your post on phone before publishing

**H2: Your Action Plan (100 words)**
- Audit one post this week
- Fix these 5 things
- Track traffic changes

**Conclusion (100 words)**
- Recap: These mistakes are common, fixable
- Question: "Which mistake are you making? (I'm guilty of #2.)"
- CTA: "Subscribe for weekly SEO tips that actually work."

**References (5-7 sources)**
- Google algorithm updates
- Traffic/readability studies
- Industry reports
